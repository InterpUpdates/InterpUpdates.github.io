Exp 4 — Gated-MLP role decomposition of C+/C−

Resolving Exp 3: the C+/C− activation sign is generated by w_in (input projection), not w_out.

Setup
Task. Binary sentiment classification of movie reviews (IMDB-derived). The base LLM is frozen; a linear CLS head reads the final-token hidden state and predicts positive / negative.
Spurious setups (two regimes). bad (amplify): the ordinary word "bad" is made to correlate with the negative label. zxq (construct): a non-semantic token "zxq" is injected and made perfectly correlated with the negative label in training.
Models. Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct.
Masks. Sparse per-neuron MLP masks (hard-concrete L0) are trained on the frozen base + CLS head to solve the task. All interpretation and ablation here run with these learned masks turned ON — we analyze/remove neurons on top of the mask, not instead of it.
Evaluation. Balanced 2×2 validation set (label × cue-present), 2000 rows. gap = majority-group accuracy − minority-group accuracy, where the minority groups are pos+cue and neg+no-cue. A large gap means the model is leaning on the spurious cue.
This experiment (gated-MLP role decomposition). ROTATE shows the three weight vectors split roles: w_gate = whether the neuron fires, w_in = sign of the activation, w_out = what is written. Our C+/C− buckets are an activation-sign split, and Exp 3 found the sign isn't in w_out. Here we capture the activation components a = SiLU(w_gate·x)·(w_in·x) on positive-label examples and test whether the input projection w_in·x̄ generates the C+/C− split, and whether C− neurons are suppressed because the gate switches them off (w_gate) or because the input value is negative (w_in).

Key result. The C+/C− split lives on the input side: sign(w_in·x̄) predicts the bucket for 95% of neurons (C+ has w_in·x̄>0 in 95%, C− has w_in·x̄<0 in 95%) — versus ~50% for the pure w_out weight sign (Exp 3). C− suppression is driven mainly by a negative input value (w_in·x < 0) (C+ vs C− mean SiLU(gate) 4.71 vs 4.10; mean w_in·x̄ 1.03 vs -1.49).
