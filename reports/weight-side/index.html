Experiment 3 — Weight-side (ROTATE) validation of C+/C−

Data-free: does the vocabulary geometry of the weights confirm the C+/C− split and disentangle the polysemantic neurons?

Setup
Task. Binary sentiment classification of movie reviews (IMDB-derived). The base LLM is frozen; a linear CLS head reads the final-token hidden state and predicts positive / negative.
Spurious setups (two regimes). bad (amplify): the ordinary word "bad" is made to correlate with the negative label. zxq (construct): a non-semantic token "zxq" is injected and made perfectly correlated with the negative label in training.
Models. Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct.
Masks. Sparse per-neuron MLP masks (hard-concrete L0) are trained on the frozen base + CLS head to solve the task. All interpretation and ablation here run with these learned masks turned ON — we analyze/remove neurons on top of the mask, not instead of it.
Evaluation. Balanced 2×2 validation set (label × cue-present), 2000 rows. gap = majority-group accuracy − minority-group accuracy, where the minority groups are pos+cue and neg+no-cue. A large gap means the model is leaning on the spurious cue.
This experiment (weight-side / ROTATE-inspired, data-free). No data or forward passes: we project each neuron's output weight wout (and gate weight) through the unembedding U to get a vocabulary distribution, and onto the CLS decision direction. (A) vocabulary kurtosis measures monosemanticity (high = clean single concept). (B) the sign of wout·CLS should predict the data-driven C+/C− bucket (C+ = writes toward NEG when active → positive; C− = via suppression → negative) — a weight-side validation of the C+/C− design. (C) ROTATE peels polysemantic neurons into separate high-kurtosis vocabulary channels. Caveats: vocabulary-aligned only (features orthogonal to the vocab are invisible); the non-semantic zxq cue behaves like a glitch token for this method; our readout is a 2-class CLS head, so the output side is summarized by the 1-D CLS projection rather than full vocab promotion.
