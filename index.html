Exp 5 — Are C+ and C− feature sets independent?

Exp 4 — Mask inspection: deleted/amplified neurons

Then max-activates and describes the most-deleted and most-amplified neurons (pre-mask activation) to interpret what fine-tuning removed/boosted. Exp 1 — Weight-side (ROTATE) validation of C+/C−

Data-free, weight-only analysis (ROTATE-inspired, Lin et al.). Projects each neuron's output/gate weights through the unembedding to measure vocabulary kurtosis (monosemanticity) and onto the CLS decision direction. Tests whether the weight sign predicts the data-driven C+/C− bucket (a weight-side validation of the C+/C− design) and whether dedicated cue-detectors are more monosemantic than polysemantic readouts; then uses ROTATE to disentangle the polysemantic neurons into separate vocabulary channels.

Key result: C+/C- validated by ACTIVATION not weights — clean frac_active split (C+ active ~0.9 vs C- suppressed ~0.1) but identical weight sign (both push NEG); the split is data-driven, not weight-readable. Kurtosis/logit-lens did not transfer (noisy in this setup). [Llama bad: frac_active C+0.87/C-0.10, w_out·CLS>0 for 80%/80% of C+/C-; Llama zxq: frac_active C+0.94/C-0.06, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/75% of C+/C-; Qwen bad: frac_active C+0.90/C-0.13, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/65% of C+/C-; Qwen zxq: frac_active C+0.93/C-0.08, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/75% of C+/C-]
