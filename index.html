Exp 3 — Weight-side (ROTATE) validation of C+/C−

Data-free, weight-only analysis (ROTATE-inspired, Lin et al.). Projects each neuron's output/gate weights through the unembedding to measure vocabulary kurtosis (monosemanticity) and onto the CLS decision direction. Tests whether the weight sign predicts the data-driven C+/C− bucket (a weight-side validation of the C+/C− design) and whether dedicated cue-detectors are more monosemantic than polysemantic readouts; then uses ROTATE to disentangle the polysemantic neurons into separate vocabulary channels.

Key result: C+/C- validated by ACTIVATION not weights — clean frac_active split (C+ active ~0.9 vs C- suppressed ~0.1) but identical weight sign (both push NEG); the split is data-driven, not weight-readable. Kurtosis/logit-lens did not transfer (noisy in this setup). [Llama bad: frac_active C+0.87/C-0.10, w_out·CLS>0 for 80%/80% of C+/C-; Llama zxq: frac_active C+0.94/C-0.06, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/75% of C+/C-; Qwen bad: frac_active C+0.90/C-0.13, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/65% of C+/C-; Qwen zxq: frac_active C+0.93/C-0.08, w_out·CLS>0 for 75%/75% of C+/C-]

Exp 6 — Mask inspection: deleted/amplified neurons & heavy-lifting

Inspects the trained mask M=1+z·δ per neuron: how many neurons fine-tuning deleted (M≈0), amplified (M>1), or left unchanged (M≈1). Heavy-lifting test: eval gap/accuracy with masks ON vs OFF (δ=0, base model) using the same CLS head, to see whether the classifier or the model edit drives behavior. Max-activates and describes the most-deleted and most-amplified neurons (pre-mask activation) to interpret what fine-tuning removed/boosted.

Key result: Mask edits a small fraction of neurons; heavy-lifting = masks ON vs OFF (base+same CLS). Exp 8 — Are C+ and C− feature sets independent?

Correlation, cosine similarity, and top-document overlap of activation patterns within-C+, within-C−, and across the two buckets. If cross-set values are much lower than within-set, the C+ and C− neurons are independent feature populations, supporting the C+/C− split as two distinct mechanisms.

Key result: C+/C- independence: cross-set activation correlation vs within-set (lower cross = independent).
